###### Abstract 摘要

LLMs have achieved remarkable breakthroughs in reasoning, insights, and tool use, but chaining these abilities into extended processes at the scale of those routinely executed by humans, organizations, and societies has remained out of reach. The models have a persistent error rate that prevents scale-up: for instance, recent experiments in the Towers of Hanoi benchmark domain showed that the process inevitably becomes derailed after at most a few hundred steps. Thus, although LLM research is often still benchmarked on tasks with relatively few dependent logical steps, there is increasing attention on the ability (or inability) of LLMs to perform long range tasks. This paper describes MAKER, the first system that successfully solves a task with over one million LLM steps with zero errors, and, in principle, scales far beyond this level. The approach relies on an extreme decomposition of a task into subtasks, each of which can be tackled by focused microagents. The high level of modularity resulting from the decomposition allows error correction to be applied at each step through an efficient multi-agent voting scheme. This combination of extreme decomposition and error correction makes scaling possible. Thus, the results suggest that instead of relying on continual improvement of current LLMs, massively decomposed agentic processes (MDAPs) may provide a way to efficiently solve problems at the level of organizations and societies.  
LLM 在推理、洞见和工具使用方面取得了显著突破，但要将这些能力串联成像人类、组织和社会常规执行的那种规模的长期过程，仍然遥不可及。模型存在持续的错误率，阻碍了规模化：例如，最近在汉诺塔基准领域的实验显示，该过程最多在几百步后就不可避免地偏离轨道。因此，尽管 LLM 研究通常仍以逻辑依赖步骤较少的任务为基准，但人们对 LLM 执行远程任务的能力（或无能）日益关注。本文介绍了 MAKER，这是首个成功以零错误解答超过一百万个 LLM 步骤任务的系统，并且原则上可以远超此规模。该方法依赖于将任务极端分解为子任务，每个子任务由专注的微代理（microagents）处理。由分解带来的高度模块化允许通过高效的多代理投票机制在每一步应用错误纠正。 这种极端分解与错误纠正相结合的方式使得扩展成为可能。因此，结果表明，与其依赖对当前 LLMs 的持续改进，不如采用大规模分解的主体化过程（MDAPs），这可能为在组织和社会层面上高效解决问题提供一种途径。

## 1 Introduction 1 引言

Technological achievements of advanced societies are built on the capacity to reliably execute tasks with vast numbers of steps. Whether constructing a skyscraper, airplane, particle accelerator, or iPhone (which relies on tangible contributions from $\approx 1$ B people in an enormous supply chain spanning hundreds of suppliers across multiple countries \[wikipedia\_apple\_supply\_chain, apple\_supplier\_list\]), running a hospital or medical research organization, processing tax returns and delivering social benefits at a national scale, or even something as seemingly simple as producing a loaf of bread \[VegPatchKitchen\_BreadStages\], the precise execution of detailed plans and policies is critical to producing high-value outcomes and maintaining societal trust, as the impact of an error in such tasks can range from inconvenience to economic harm to physical harm to death.  
先进社会的技术成就建立在可靠执行包含大量步骤任务的能力之上。无论是建造摩天大楼、飞机、粒子加速器，还是 iPhone（其依赖于跨越数百家供应商、分布于多个国家的庞大供应链中 $\approx 1$ 亿人的实实在在贡献 \[wikipedia\_apple\_supply\_chain, apple\_supplier\_list\]），运营医院或医学研究机构、处理纳税申报并在国家层面发放社会福利，还是看似简单的事情如烤制一条面包 \[VegPatchKitchen\_BreadStages\]，详尽计划和政策的精确执行对于产出高价值成果和维护社会信任至关重要，因为此类任务中发生错误的影响可能从带来不便、经济损失到造成身体伤害甚至死亡。

Large language models (LLMs) are increasingly inserted into large and complex real-world processes like these. To maximize the benefit of using LLMs in these roles, it is critical to understand the limits of where and how LLMs can be reliably deployed. This paper focuses on the question of how/whether LLMs can execute large tasks with extreme precision, e.g., when a 1% per-step error rate is not acceptable.  
大型语言模型（LLMs）正越来越多地被嵌入到此类大型且复杂的现实流程中。为了最大化在这些角色中使用 LLMs 的效益，关键是要理解 LLMs 可以可靠部署的范围与方式的限制。本文聚焦于 LLMs 如何/是否能够以极高精度执行大规模任务的问题，例如当每步 1%的错误率不可接受时。

This question is investigated by applying LLM-based reasoning to a task whose solution requires more than one million LLM steps with zero errors. The Towers of Hanoi problem, recently proposed as a benchmark for LLM reasoning \[shojaee2025illusion\], provides such a task. Most benchmarks for evaluating the quality of LLMs use independent examples, each requiring not many more than a few dependent logical execution steps \[patel2024multistep\], with a resulting score like accuracy averaged over all examples. Such a benchmark might be considered solved if the accuracy is 99%. However, a system with a 1% per-step error rate is expected to fail after only 100 steps of a million-step task. Therefore, solving a million-step task with zero errors requires a fundamentally different approach.  
通过将基于 LLM 的推理应用于一个其解法需要超过一百万个 LLM 步骤且零错误的任务来研究这个问题。最近被提出作为 LLM 推理基准的汉诺塔问题\[shojaee2025illusion\]就提供了这样的任务。用于评估 LLM 质量的大多数基准使用独立的示例，每个示例需要的不超过几步相关的逻辑执行步骤\[patel2024multistep\]，其结果分数通常是对所有示例求平均的准确率。若某个基准的准确率为 99%，则可能被认为已被解决。然而，对于一个每步错误率为 1%的系统而言，在一百万步任务中仅 100 步后就预计会失败。因此，要在一百万步的任务中实现零错误，需要采取根本不同的方法。

Such an approach is proposed in this paper: Massively decomposed agentic processes (MDAPs). The main contributions of this paper are as follows:  
本文提出了这样一种方法：大规模分解的代理过程（MDAPs）。本文的主要贡献如下：

- A design of the MDAP framework, which consists of three core components: (1) decomposition into minimal subtasks; (2) error correction based on subtask-level voting; and (3) red-flagging to reduce correlated errors.
	  
	• 设计了 MDAP 框架，包含三个核心组成部分：（1）将任务分解为最小子任务；（2）基于子任务级投票的错误修正；（3）通过红旗标记来减少相关错误。
- A formalization of this framework that yields scaling laws, e.g., how probability of success and expected cost change w.r.t. the number of total steps and level of task decomposition. Under this formalization we find effective scaling under extreme decomposition and infeasibility without it.
	  
	• 对该框架进行形式化，得出缩放定律，例如成功概率和预期成本如何随总步骤数和任务分解层级的变化而变化。在该形式化下，我们发现极端分解下存在有效的缩放性，而没有分解则不可行。
- The empirical applications of the framework to successfully solve a task with over one million steps with zero errors. One main takeaway is that ‘state-of-the-art’ reasoning models are not required; relatively small non-reasoning models suffice.
	  
	• 将该框架在实验中应用于成功解决一个超过一百万步且零错误的任务。一个主要结论是，不需要“最先进”的推理模型；相对较小的非推理模型就足够。

This paper provides a first implementation of the MDAP framework: MAKER (for M aximal A gentic decomposition, first-to-ahead-by- K E rror correction, and R ed-flagging), and evaluates it in the Towers of Hanoi domain. MAKER is a system of *agents* in which each agent is assigned a single subtask to solve. In other words, the *role* of each agent is defined by the subtask it is assigned. As advocated for in prior work \[meyersonposition\], by avoiding anthropomorphizing agents (i.e., by assigning them human-level roles) and instead assigning them tiny ‘micro-roles’, it is possible to exploit the inherent machine-like nature of LLMs. It may then be possible to take advantage of the kinds of error-correction methods that have been essential to scaling in many domains of computing, i.e., by assigning multiple agents to independently solve the same subtask.  
本文提供了 MDAP 框架的首个实现：MAKER（用于最大能动性分解、先行 K 步误差修正和红旗标记），并在河内塔问题域中对其进行了评估。MAKER 是一个代理系统，其中每个代理被分配去解决单一子任务。换言之，每个代理的角色由其被分配的子任务决定。正如先前工作\[meyersonposition\]所倡导的，通过避免将代理拟人化（即避免赋予其人类级别的角色），而改为分配微小的“微角色”，可以利用 LLMs 的内在机器特性。随后可能有机会利用在许多计算领域扩展时至关重要的那类错误修正方法，例如通过分配多个代理独立解决相同的子任务。

The results demonstrate an instance of multi-agent advantage (akin to quantum advantage \[harrow2017quantum\]), that is, a solution to a problem that is not solvable by a monolithic single-agent system. This demonstration sets the stage for a new paradigm of scaling AI: instead of relying on continual improvement of simple underlying LLMs, more powerful AI is achieved through massively decomposed agentic processes (MDAPs).  
结果展示了一个多智能体优势的实例（类似于量子优越性 \[harrow2017quantum\]），即对一个单一整体式智能体系统无法解决的问题的解决方案。该演示为一种新的 AI 扩展范式奠定了基础：与其依赖对简单底层 LLMs 的持续改进，不如通过大规模分解的智能体过程（MDAPs）来实现更强大的 AI。

## 2 Background 2 背景

While current LLM agents suffer from catastrophic errors on large tasks, there may be an opportunity for a multi-agent approach that decomposes the tasks into small steps. Error correction is critical for this process, as it is in many complex digital and biological systems.  
尽管当前的 LLM 代理在大型任务上会出现灾难性错误，但通过将任务分解为小步骤的多代理方法可能存在机会。错误修正对这一过程至关重要，就像在许多复杂的数字和生物系统中一样。

### 2.1 Large Agentic LLM Tasks2.1 大型能动 LLM 任务

As large language models have improved, increasing consideration has been given towards real world economic tasks that require multi-step, long horizon reasoning \[kwa2025\]. Research in this direction has repeatedly confirmed an inherent property of LLMs: their performance deteriorates significantly (and often exponentially) with the length of the task horizon, regardless of task complexity \[schaeffer2023, Dziriexp\]. This observation has led to the recent focus on the ability (and failure) of LLMs to *execute*, i.e., failing to complete many-step tasks, even when a correct plan to follow is explicitly provided to them \[sinha2025\]. While this work identified a fundamental liability of LLMs in long-horizon execution, it also presented an opportunity: Even small improvements in individual subtask performance could lead to exponential improvements in achievable task lengths \[sinha2025\].  
随着大型语言模型的改进，社区对需要多步骤、长时间跨度推理的现实经济任务给予了越来越多的关注 \[kwa2025\]。该方向的研究反复证实了一个 LLMs 的固有特性：无论任务复杂度如何，其性能都会随着任务时间跨度的增加而显著下降（且常呈指数级下降）\[schaeffer2023, Dziriexp\]。这一观察促使近期研究聚焦于 LLMs 的执行能力（以及其失败），即在明确提供正确执行计划的情况下，LLMs 仍无法完成多步骤任务的现象 \[sinha2025\]。虽然这项工作揭示了 LLMs 在长时间跨度执行上的根本缺陷，但它也带来了机会：即使对单个子任务性能的微小改进，也可能带来可完成任务长度的指数级提升 \[sinha2025\]。

At the same time, recent theoretical work has claimed that decomposing large tasks into the smallest possible subtasks can have substantial efficiency benefits \[meyersonposition\]. The rise of decomposing tasks into subtasks solvable by focused “small language model” (SLM) agents in industry, motivated by both reliability and cost \[belcak2025smalllanguagemodelsfuture\], as well as the burgeoning study of multi-agent LLM systems in academia \[guo2024largelanguagemodelbased, wang2024llm\_agents\_survey\], provides evidence for the practicality of this idea. This paper continues this line of work. It is based on the premise that tasks should be broken up into the smallest possible elements, so that an LLM agent can focus on them one step at a time, improving per step error rate, and thus enabling scaling, reliability, and efficiency in the limit. Critical to the feasibility of such an approach is the granularity of the decomposition, i.e., what defines a single *step*. Since this paper focuses on execution, it is assumed the definition of step is given a priori; an orthogonal open question is how to automatically discover optimal decompositions \[russell1991principles, horvitz2021ideal\]. Informally, the main condition required for the methods in Section [3](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.09030v1#S3 "3 Methods ‣ Solving a Million-Step LLM Task with Zero Errors") to work is that steps are small enough that, for each step, a correct solution is likely to be sampled, and no incorrect solution is more likely.  
与此同时，最近的理论研究声称，将大型任务分解为尽可能小的子任务可以带来显著的效率收益\[meyersonposition\]。在工业界，将任务分解为由专注的“小型语言模型”（SLM）代理可解决的子任务的兴起，既受可靠性又受成本的驱动\[belcak2025smalllanguagemodelsfuture\]，以及学术界对多代理 LLM 系统日益增多的研究\[guo2024largelanguagemodelbased, wang2024llm\_agents\_survey\]，为这一想法的可行性提供了证据。本文延续了这一研究方向。本文的前提是，应将任务拆分为尽可能小的要素，以便 LLM 代理能够一次专注于一步，从而提高每一步的错误率表现（即降低错误率），从而在极限上实现可扩展性、可靠性和效率。此类方法可行性的关键在于分解的粒度，即什么构成单一步骤。由于本文聚焦于执行层面，假设步骤的定义是先验给定的；一个正交的开放问题是如何自动发现最优分解\[russell1991principles, horvitz2021ideal\]。 非正式地说，第 3 节方法能够奏效的主要条件是每一步都足够小，以致于对于每个步骤，采样到正确解的概率很高，并且没有任何错误解更有可能被采样。

Now, when an agentic system is applied to a long and expensive multi-step task, there is a natural desire to extract relevant information from responses even when formatted incorrectly. As a result, substantial work has gone in to generating correctly structured output from an LLM. Grammar-constrained (JSON/CFG) decoding reliably enforces structure and often improves downstream pipelines \[geng2025jsonschemabench, openai2024structured\], LLMs are fine-tuned to get the format right \[grattafiori2024llama3herdmodels, qiu2025evolution\], sampling is performed in a way that only tokens respecting the required format are selected \[chen2022relation\], and Python packages are developed dedicated to fixing an LLM’s output post-hoc so that it can be parsed in a meaningful way \[pydantic, json\_repair, guardrails\_ai\]. However, as described in Section [3.3](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.09030v1#S3.SS3 "3.3 Red-Flagging: Recognizing Signs of Unreliability ‣ 3 Methods ‣ Solving a Million-Step LLM Task with Zero Errors"), when an agent makes an error in the output format, this error may indicate that its other reasoning is wrong as well. When a task has been broken into tiny pieces, this property may be exploited to mitigate errors.  
现在，当将具有代理能力的系统应用于耗时且昂贵的多步骤任务时，人们自然希望即使在响应格式不正确时也能从中提取相关信息。因此，已经投入大量工作以从 LLM 生成正确结构的输出。受语法约束的（JSON/CFG）解码可靠地强制执行结构，并且常常改善下游管道\[geng2025jsonschemabench, openai2024structured\]；对 LLMs 进行微调以使格式正确\[grattafiori2024llama3herdmodels, qiu2025evolution\]；以仅选择遵守所需格式的标记的方式进行抽样\[chen2022relation\]；并且开发了专门用于事后修复 LLM 输出以便能够以有意义的方式解析的 Python 包\[pydantic, json\_repair, guardrails\_ai\]。然而，如第 3.3 节所述，当一个代理在输出格式上出现错误时，该错误可能表明其其他推理也有问题。当任务被拆分成微小步骤时，这一特性可以被利用来减轻错误。

### 2.2 Error Correction 2.2 错误更正

Error correction is a critical capability across many important areas of computing, including communication \[clark1981error, lin2004error\], memory storage \[chen1984error\], and quantum computing \[roffe2019quantum\]. Error correction makes it possible to pretend that digital communication and classical computation are deterministic, when in fact, single bits are getting lost and flipped all the time \[normand1996single, wang2008single\]. Similarly, improved error correction is the single most important ingredient to achieving scalable quantum computing \[fowler2012surface\_code\]. In biological systems, error correction is critical to large processes growing and persisting over time. Error correction is necessary both at the population level, e.g., through the error-correcting effects of recombination \[otto2002resolving\], and at the individual level, e.g., the cancer-fighting ability of mammals. At the individual level, it correlates highly with lifespan and body size (i.e., scale), with elephants showing the most impressive resistance \[abegglen2015potential\]. LLMs now serve as the basis of another substrate of computing, *linguistic computing*, whose constituent processes are *language-based algorithms* (LbAs) \[meyersonposition, chen2024design\]. It should then come as no surprise that error correction is critical to achieving LbAs that scale, mitigating for the inherent nondeterminism that results from producing language by pulling from a probability distribution.  
错误更正（纠错）是在许多重要计算领域中的一项关键能力，包括通信 \[clark1981error, lin2004error\]、存储器存储 \[chen1984error\] 和量子计算 \[roffe2019quantum\]。错误更正使得在实际上单个位经常丢失和翻转的情况下，我们可以假装数字通信和经典计算是确定性的 \[normand1996single, wang2008single\]。同样，改进的错误更正是实现可扩展量子计算的最重要的因素 \[fowler2012surface\_code\]。在生物系统中，错误更正对于大型过程的增长和长期维持至关重要。错误更正既在群体层面是必要的，例如通过重组的纠错效应 \[otto2002resolving\]，也在个体层面是必要的，例如哺乳动物的抗癌能力。在个体层面，它与寿命和体型（即规模）高度相关，其中大象表现出最显著的抗性 \[abegglen2015potential\]。 LLMs 现在成为另一种计算基质的基础——语言计算，其组成过程是基于语言的算法（LbAs）\[meyersonposition, chen2024design\]。 因此，纠错对于实现可扩展的 LbAs 至关重要，这一点不足为奇，它可以缓解从概率分布中抽取语言所产生的固有非确定性。

Many possible LbA error correction methods can be derived from instances in other fields \[shannon1948\]. One way to reduce errors is for an LLM to reflect on its output and explicitly correct any error it sees \[manakul2023selfcheckgpt\]. Another approach is to quantify and exploit LLM uncertainty explicitly \[xue2023dynamicvoting, xin2024semantic\]. For example, work on *semantic density* shows that the semantic content most consistently sampled from an LLM for a given prompt is more likely to be correct than a greedy decoding \[xin2024semantic\]. This promise of semantic consistency in sampling makes a third, simpler, approach possible: voting, or *ensembling*, which has been a core machine learning technique for decades \[opitz1999popular, mienye2022survey, ganaie2022ensemble\], and is now commonly used to boost the accuracy of LLM-based systems \[Trad2025voting\]. To date, ensembling has mostly been implemented in LLMs at an action level far above that of a single minimal step. For example, state-of-the-art LLM-based coding systems often use a majority vote of outputs of complete programs that are each a candidate solution to a coding challenge \[li2022alphacode, wang2022selfconsistency\]. However, when scaling to tasks with thousands or millions of dependent steps, the level of granularity at which error correction is applied is critical, as will be shown in Section [3](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.09030v1#S3 "3 Methods ‣ Solving a Million-Step LLM Task with Zero Errors").  
可以从其他领域的实例中推导出许多可能的 LbA 错误纠正方法 \[shannon1948\]。减少错误的一种方法是让 LLM 对其输出进行反思并明确纠正它看到的任何错误 \[manakul2023selfcheckgpt\]。另一种方法是明确量化并利用 LLM 的不确定性 \[xue2023dynamicvoting, xin2024semantic\]。例如，关于语义密度的研究表明，对于给定提示，从 LLM 中最一致抽样得到的语义内容比贪心解码更有可能是正确的 \[xin2024semantic\]。这种在抽样中语义一致性的前景使第三种更简单的方法成为可能：投票或集成，这已是几十年来的核心机器学习技术 \[opitz1999popular, mienye2022survey, ganaie2022ensemble\]，现在通常用于提升基于 LLM 的系统的准确性 \[Trad2025voting\]。迄今为止，集成大多在远高于单一步骤的动作层面上在 LLM 中实现。例如，最先进的基于 LLM 的编程系统通常对作为编码挑战候选解的完整程序的输出进行多数投票 \[li2022alphacode, wang2022selfconsistency\]。 然而，当扩展到包含数千或数百万个相互依赖步骤的任务时，如第 3 节所示，错误修正所施加的粒度水平至关重要。

### 2.3 Motivating Challenge Domain: Towers of Hanoi2.3 激发性挑战领域：河内塔

Towers of Hanoi was recently introduced as a test domain for investigating the capabilities and limitations of state-of-the-art LLM reasoning models \[shojaee2025illusion\]. This benchmark is based on the classic game in which there are three pegs and $D$ disks, and the goal is to move all disks from the first to the third peg, moving only one disk at a time, and maintaining the condition that a larger disk never sits atop a smaller one \[hinz2013tower\]. In the benchmark, an LLM system is asked to produce a sequence of moves $(m_{i}=[d_{i},s_{i},t_{i}])_{i=1}^{n}$ whose execution completes the task, where the $i$ th move is executed by moving disk number $d_{i}\in\{1,...,D\}$ from source peg $s_{i}\in\{0,1,2\}$ to target peg $t_{i}\in\{0,1,2\}$ . The problem scales naturally to enormous numbers of required steps by simply adding more disks, since the optimal number of steps to complete the task is $2^{D}-1$ . For example, solving Towers of Hanoi with ten disks takes just over a thousand steps, and with twenty disks just over a million steps. In its most famous (mythological) incarnation, monks work continuously on an instance with 64 disks, which is expected to take around 585 billion years, at which point the universe will end \[moscovich20011\].  
汉诺塔最近被引入作为一个测试领域，用于研究最先进的 LLM 推理模型的能力和局限性\[shojaee2025illusion\]。该基准基于经典游戏：有三根柱子和 $D$ 个盘子，目标是将所有盘子从第一根柱子移动到第三根柱子，每次只能移动一个盘子，并且始终保持大盘子不能放在小盘子之上\[hinz2013tower\]。在该基准中，要求一个 LLM 系统生成一系列移动 $(m_{i}=[d_{i},s_{i},t_{i}])_{i=1}^{n}$ ，其执行能完成任务，其中第 $i$ 步通过将编号为 $d_{i}\in\{1,...,D\}$ 的盘子从源柱子 $s_{i}\in\{0,1,2\}$ 移到目标柱子 $t_{i}\in\{0,1,2\}$ 来执行。通过简单地增加盘子的数量，这个问题可以自然地扩展到需要极大量步骤的情况，因为完成任务的最优步数是 $2^{D}-1$ 。例如，解决十个盘子的汉诺塔需要刚好超过一千步，而二十个盘子则需要刚好超过一百万步。在其最著名（神话般的）版本中，僧侣们持续工作于一个有 64 个盘子的实例，预计大约需要 5850 亿年，此时宇宙将结束\[moscovich20011\]。

Performance of state-of-the-art LLMs degrades catastrophically on this benchmark: They are able to complete the task with a high success rate up until five or six disks, after which the success rate plummets to zero \[shojaee2025illusion\]. What this degradation means with respect to whether or to what extent an LLM is really ‘thinking’ or ‘reasoning’ is up for philosophical debate and is outside the scope of this paper \[varela2025rethinking, khan2025comment, opus2025illusion\]. However, this result made it clear that the reliability of state-of-the-art LLMs is fundamentally limited: If they need to complete every step correctly in order to solve a task, after a certain number of steps they will almost surely fail as a result of an underlying propensity to make errors, even when the answer should be obvious. While an error rate of 1-in-1,000 seems low, and would be great on a traditional LLM benchmark, on a task that requires successful execution of thousands of steps in a row, such a system results in inevitable failure.  
最先进的 LLMs 在该基准测试上的性能出现灾难性下降：它们在五到六个盘子之前能够以很高的成功率完成任务，但之后成功率骤降至零 \[shojaee2025illusion\]。这种性能下降在多大程度上说明 LLM 是否真正“思考”或“推理”，属于哲学讨论范畴，且超出本文范围 \[varela2025rethinking, khan2025comment, opus2025illusion\]。然而，这一结果清楚表明最先进 LLMs 的可靠性存在根本性限制：如果它们必须每一步都正确才能解决任务，那么在一定步数之后，由于其潜在的犯错倾向，它们几乎肯定会失败，即使答案应该显而易见。尽管每一千次出错一次的错误率看起来很低，并且在传统的 LLM 基准上会表现良好，但在需要连续成功执行数千步的任务中，这样的系统必然会失败。

Two critiques of Towers of Hanoi as a benchmark should be addressed upfront. First, one could argue that it is not an ideal LLM task since one could write code to solve the problem, and optimal algorithms are known \[hinz2013tower\]. True, but producing solutions is not the point: instead, the domain provides an ideal testbed for investigating the capacity of LLM-based systems to scale their inherent intelligence to increasingly large numbers of steps. Second, one could argue that this problem is too hard, since large real-world tasks might allow for a handful of errors without catastrophic results \[simon1957models\]. However, focusing on a case where no error can be tolerated forces us to pursue the elimination of any kind of error that is likely to arise on a long timescale, and this focus can lead to insights and practical methods that might otherwise be overlooked. There also are real-world safety-critical systems where no error can be tolerated \[kremer1993ring\]. Therefore, as LLM-based systems become ubiquitous in real-world decision making processes, it is essential these systems can reliably make decisions without error. Thus, the problem provides an ideal testbed for developing techniques that will be critical to scaling LLM-based systems to one million steps and beyond.  
关于将河内塔作为基准测试的两个批评应当在一开始就予以回应。首先，有人可能会认为这不是一个理想的 LLM 任务，因为人们可以编写代码来解决这个问题，并且已知有最优算法\[hinz2013tower\]。这虽然属实，但生成解答并非重点：相反，该领域为研究基于 LLM 的系统能否将其内在智能扩展到越来越多步骤提供了理想的试验场。其次，有人可能会认为这个问题过于困难，因为现实世界中的大型任务可能允许少量错误而不会导致灾难性后果\[simon1957models\]。然而，聚焦于一个不能容忍任何错误的情形，迫使我们致力于消除在长时间尺度上可能出现的任何类型的错误，而这种聚焦可以带来可能被忽视的洞见和实用方法。现实中也存在不能容忍任何错误的安全关键系统\[kremer1993ring\]。因此，随着基于 LLM 的系统在现实决策过程中变得无处不在，这些系统必须能够可靠地做出无错误的决策。 因此，这个问题为开发对将基于 LLM 的系统扩展到一百万步及更高步数至关重要的技术提供了理想的试验平台。

## 3 Methods 3 方法

MAKER involves three main ingredients (Figure [2](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.09030v1#S2.F2 "Figure 2 ‣ 2.3 Motivating Challenge Domain: Towers of Hanoi ‣ 2 Background ‣ Solving a Million-Step LLM Task with Zero Errors")): (1) Decomposing a task into the smallest possible subtasks; (2) exploiting the modularity of such a decomposition to implement efficient error correction; and (3) “red-flagging” LLM outputs, i.e., discarding outputs whose structure suggests increased risk of errors, particularly correlated errors. These three components are detailed in the next three subsections. Together, they make it possible to efficiently increase the probability of success across all steps to a level such that the entire process is likely to succeed.  
MAKER 包含三个主要要素（图 2）：(1) 将任务分解为尽可能小的子任务；(2) 利用这种分解的模块化来实现高效的错误纠正；以及 (3) 对 LLM 输出进行“红旗警示”，即丢弃那些其结构表明错误风险增加，特别是相关错误风险增加的输出。这三个组成部分将在接下来的三小节中详细说明。三者结合，使得有可能高效地将所有步骤的成功概率提升到一个水平，从而使整个过程很可能成功。

Because LLMs are auto-regressive, when generating the $i$ th action, a single agent $M$ is increasingly burdened by the context produced in generating actions $a_{1},\ldots,a_{i-1}$ . Therefore, as the context increases, its outputs become increasingly unreliable \[du2025contextlengthhurtsllm\]. However with MAD, an agent’s context is limited to an amount of information sufficient to execute its single assigned step, allowing it to focus on its assigned role and avoid confusion that can creep in from irrelevant context. This focus also allows the use of smaller LLMs with more limited context sizes.  
因为 LLMs 是自回归的，在生成第 $i$ 个动作时，单个代理 $M$ 会随着生成动作 $a_{1},\ldots,a_{i-1}$ 时产生的上下文而承受越来越大的负担。因此，随着上下文的增多，其输出变得越来越不可靠 \[du2025contextlengthhurtsllm\]。然而在 MAD 下，代理的上下文被限制在足以执行其单个分配步骤的信息量，允许其专注于所分配的角色，避免因无关上下文而产生的混淆。这种专注也允许使用上下文大小更受限的更小型 LLMs。

One might argue that this decomposition might improve the reliability of any given LLM call, but by decomposing the task into $s$ independent calls, there are now $s$ possible points of failure, instead of just one. ### 3.2 First-to-ahead-by-kk Voting and Scaling Laws3.2First-to-ahead-by- kk 投票与规模定律

For simplicity, the error correction in this paper uses the statistical power of independent samples from a stochastic process (here an LLM). To determine a winner from these samples, a *first-to-ahead-by- $k$* voting process is used, motivated by the optimality of such an approach in the sequential probability ratio test (SPRT) \[wald2004sequential, lee2025consol\]. Many improvements are possible beyond this first implementation. For example, in the experiments in this paper, exact matches between actions are required, but in general, a classification function could be used to determine semantically equivalent outputs (e.g., implemented by an LLM, see Section [5](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.09030v1#S5 "5 Discussion and Future Work ‣ Solving a Million-Step LLM Task with Zero Errors")).  
为简便起见，本文中的纠错方法利用了来自随机过程（此处为 LLM）的独立样本的统计能力。为了从这些样本中确定优胜者，使用了先到领先 $k$ 的投票过程，这一方法的动机来源于顺序概率比检验（SPRT）中此类方法的最优性 \[wald2004sequential, lee2025consol\]。在这一初始实现之外还有许多可改进之处。例如，在本文的实验中，动作之间要求完全匹配，但一般情况下可以使用分类函数来判定语义上等价的输出（例如，由 LLM 实现，见第 5 节）。

Concretely, given an LLM $M$ , candidate samples are drawn for a subtask (Eq. [2](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.09030v1#S3.E2 "In 3.1 Maximal Agentic Decomposition ‣ 3 Methods ‣ Solving a Million-Step LLM Task with Zero Errors") & [3](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.09030v1#S3.E3 "In 3.1 Maximal Agentic Decomposition ‣ 3 Methods ‣ Solving a Million-Step LLM Task with Zero Errors")) until one has been sampled $k$ times more than any other (Alg. [2](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.09030v1#alg2 "Algorithm 2 ‣ Figure 2 ‣ 2.3 Motivating Challenge Domain: Towers of Hanoi ‣ 2 Background ‣ Solving a Million-Step LLM Task with Zero Errors")). ### 3.3 Red-Flagging: Recognizing Signs of Unreliability3.3 标红提示：识别不可靠迹象

Since $p$ plays such an important role in the cost of the system, when possible, it is worth taking practical measures to push it higher. The simple premise is that “bad” behaviors are correlated in LLMs, so if an LLM produces a response that signals pathological behavior, the response should be flagged and simply discarded. Since in MAD each agent is responsible for only a single step, each step is not too expensive, and it can be discarded and a new action resampled (i.e., by making another agent call). In this paper, two signs of unreliability are used as red flags: (1) overly long responses, and (2) incorrectly formatted responses. The hypothesis is that not only will discarding flagged respones increase $p$ , it will also meaningfully reduce *correlated errors*, since both flag types indicate that the LLM has been conditioned into a strange starting point before sampling. It is simple to detect these signs and discard their responses, but, since the paper focuses on understanding the impact on the expected cost of highly scaled long-horizon tasks, it is worth elaborating on the motivation and impact of this implementation choice.  
既然 $p$ 在系统成本中起着如此重要的作用，在可能的情况下，采取实际措施提高它是值得的。简单的前提出发：在 LLMs 中“有害”行为是相关联的，因此如果一个 LLM 产生的响应表明病态行为，则该响应应被标记并直接丢弃。由于在 MAD 中每个代理只负责单一步骤，每一步的成本并不高，并且可以丢弃该步骤并重新采样一个新动作（即再次调用代理）。在本文中，使用了两种不可靠性的迹象作为红旗：（1）过长的响应，（2）格式错误的响应。假设是不仅丢弃被标记的响应会提高 $p$ ，还会显著减少相关错误，因为这两种标记类型都表明在采样之前 LLM 已被置于一个异常的起始点。检测这些迹象并丢弃相应的响应很简单，但由于本文侧重于理解对大规模长程任务期望成本的影响，因此值得详述这一实现选择的动机和影响。

Consistent with observations in prior work that longer answers tend to have more errors \[liu2024lostinthemiddle\], preliminary experiments for this paper showed that once an LLM gets initially confused, it can go off the rails and over-analyze a situation in a cycle of self-destruction. In MAD, each agent’s role is highly focused and relatively simple; if an agent is doing too much work to figure out its answer, it is likely to be confused and missing the point, and therefore more likely to give an incorrect answer. Even if the success rate is increased only from 99% to 99.9%, such an increase can have a large impact when the number of steps is large.  
与先前研究中观察到的结论一致：较长的回答往往错误更多\[liu2024lostinthemiddle\]，本论文的初步实验表明，一旦一个 LLM 最初产生困惑，它可能会偏离正轨，陷入过度分析的自我毁灭循环。在 MAD 中，每个代理的角色高度集中且相对简单；如果某个代理为弄清答案而承担过多工作，它很可能会感到困惑、偏离要点，因此更容易给出错误答案。即使成功率仅从 99%提高到 99.9%，当步骤数量很大时，这样的提高也会产生巨大影响。

Similarly, preliminary experiments showed that when an agent produces an answer in an incorrect format, it is more likely to have become confused at some point on the way to that answer. So, instead of trying to fix the format of the answer in some way, the detection of the incorrect format can be flagged and the sample discarded.  
类似地，初步实验表明，当一个代理生成的答案格式不正确时，该代理在生成该答案的过程中更有可能在某个时刻产生了混乱。因此，与其尝试以某种方式修复答案的格式，不如将格式不正确的检测标记出来并丢弃该样本。

### 4.1 Setup 4.1 设置

The implementation of MAKER for this problem was derived from the single-agent approach introduced in prior work \[shojaee2025illusion\]. The single-agent prompts were modified so that each agent knows that it must only perform a single step of the problem, i.e., to move a single disk.  
用于解决此问题的 MAKER 实现源自先前工作中提出的单智能体方法 \[shojaee2025illusion\]。对单智能体提示进行了修改，使得每个智能体都知道它只需执行问题的单一步骤，即移动一个圆盘。

For efficiency, and to focus the agents as much as possible, each agent is given the minimal context it needs to perform its single step. In the case of Tower of Hanoi, everything the agent needs to know is the overall strategy and the current state of the problem, i.e., the configuration of disks. As in prior work \[sinha2025, shojaee2025illusion\], the overall strategy is provided in the prompt for each agent. The strategy works for any even number of disks and is the one most often suggested by the LLMs themselves when no strategy is provided a priori. (Appendix [C](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.09030v1#A3 "Appendix C Prompts and Parsers ‣ Solving a Million-Step LLM Task with Zero Errors")). This design choice effectively isolates the ability of agents to *execute* clear instructions from the ability of LLMs to have *insights* about how tasks should be solved (Section  [5](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.09030v1#S5 "5 Discussion and Future Work ‣ Solving a Million-Step LLM Task with Zero Errors"); see also \[sinha2025\]). Both insight and execution are essential to the capabilities of LLMs, but often they are entangled in experiments, making it difficult to identify the source of failure. Focusing on execution makes it possible to pursue the goal of finding minimal conditions for scaling LLM systems with respect to the number of steps.  
为了提高效率并尽可能集中代理的注意力，每个代理只被提供执行其单步操作所需的最小上下文。在河内塔的例子中，代理需要了解的所有内容是整体策略和问题的当前状态，即圆盘的配置。与之前的工作\[sinha2025, shojaee2025illusion\]一样，整体策略在每个代理的提示中提供。该策略适用于任何偶数个圆盘，并且是在事先未给出策略时，LLMs 自己最常建议的策略。（附录 C）。这一设计选择有效地将代理执行明确指令的能力与 LLMs 对如何解决任务的洞见能力隔离开来（第 5 节；另见\[sinha2025\]）。洞见和执行对于 LLMs 的能力都是必要的，但在实验中它们常常纠缠在一起，导致难以识别失败的根源。关注执行使得有可能追求关于随着步骤数增加时扩展 LLM 系统的最小条件的目标。

The full agent prompt template is given in Appendix [C](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.09030v1#A3 "Appendix C Prompts and Parsers ‣ Solving a Million-Step LLM Task with Zero Errors"). Given the current state (i.e., configuration of disks) and prior move (which disk was moved from where to where), each agent is asked to provide the next move and the resulting next state. Unlike in the single-agent case, where only the sequence of moves needs to be produced, in the MAD case each agent must produce the resulting state, since this is critical information to be fed to the next agent. Each agent is asked to format its answers as “ move = <move> ” and “ next\_state = <next\_state> ”. Superficially, the requirement to produce the next state along with the action creates even more potential points of failure beyond the single-agent case, but, as it turns out, any drawbacks are overcome by the advantages of extreme decomposition and error correction.  
完整的代理提示模板见附录 C。给定当前状态（即，盘子的配置）和先前的移动（哪枚盘子从哪里移到了哪里），每个代理都被要求给出下一步移动及由此产生的下一状态。与单代理情况只需生成移动序列不同，在多代理分解（MAD）情况下每个代理都必须生成结果状态，因为这是传递给下一个代理的关键信息。每个代理被要求将其回答格式化为“move = <move>”和“next\_state = <next\_state>”。表面上，看似在动作的同时还要求生成下一状态会比单代理情况引入更多潜在失败点，但事实证明，任何缺点都被极端分解和错误修正的优势所克服。

### 4.2 Estimating single-step success rates4.2 估计单步成功率

Running an LLM-based system at the scale of a million steps is expensive. It is thus desirable to calibrate the parameters of the system and estimate the success rate and cost before any large experiments are run. Equation [18](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.09030v1#S3.E18 "In 3.2 First-to-ahead-by-𝑘 Voting and Scaling Laws ‣ 3 Methods ‣ Solving a Million-Step LLM Task with Zero Errors") provides such a calibration and estimation method. Key to this estimation is the per-step success rate $p$ , which depends on the underlying LLM used.  
以百万步规模运行基于 LLM 的系统代价高昂。因此在进行任何大规模实验之前，对系统参数进行校准并估计成功率和成本是很有必要的。公式 18 提供了这样的校准和估计方法。该估计的关键是每步成功率 $p$ ，其取决于所用的底层 LLM。

A straightforward way to estimate the per-step success rate is to run the system on a random subset of steps. One advantage of having the same strategy in the prompt of each agent is that the correct answer is known for each step, and, assuming every prior step is correct, the inputs are known as well. This knowledge also makes it possible to use the API to obtain a batch of answers for many steps asynchronously, thus greatly speeding up wall-clock time of experiments and reducing cost \[OpenAI\_BatchAPI\_Pricing\]. These properties are part of what makes Towers of Hanoi such a practical testbed for many-step methods. For other problems, it may not be possible to estimate $p$ as efficiently, but, in any case, it should be possible to estimate it to a practical degree.  
估计每一步成功率的一种简单方法是对随机选取的一部分步骤运行系统。将相同策略放在每个代理的提示中有一个优点：每一步的正确答案都是已知的，并且在假设之前的每一步均正确的情况下，输入也同样是已知的。这样的知识还使得可以使用 API 异步地为许多步骤获取一批答案，从而大大加快实验的实际耗时并降低成本 \[OpenAI\_BatchAPI\_Pricing\]。这些特性也是汉诺塔问题成为多步方法实用测试平台的部分原因。对于其他问题，可能无法如此高效地估计 $p$ ，但无论如何，应该可以在实用范围内对其进行估计。

These initial exploratory estimation experiments were run without red-flagging. That is, agents were given a maximum of 2048 output tokens as an initial conservative upper bound, ensuring they have plenty of space to express whatever answer they need to express. They also used a “repairing parser” (written by an LLM, Appendix [C](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.09030v1#A3 "Appendix C Prompts and Parsers ‣ Solving a Million-Step LLM Task with Zero Errors")) that attempted to correct some of the more common formatting errors in order to extract the LLM’s intended answer reliably out of its output. Importantly, since these experiments focus on the inherent generative reasoning capabilities of LLMs, they do not include LLMs that have access to auxiliary tools.  
这些初步的探索性估算实验是在不进行红旗标记的情况下运行的。也就是说，代理被给予最多 2048 个输出标记作为初始保守上限，确保它们有充足的空间表达所需的任何答案。它们还使用了一个“修复解析器”（由 LLM 编写，附录 C），试图纠正一些更常见的格式错误，以便可靠地从输出中提取 LLM 的预期答案。重要的是，由于这些实验关注 LLM 固有的生成推理能力，因此不包括能够使用辅助工具的 LLM。

Figure [6(a)](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.09030v1#S4.F6.sf1 "In Figure 6 ‣ 4.2 Estimating single-step success rates ‣ 4 Experiments ‣ Solving a Million-Step LLM Task with Zero Errors") shows the single-step error rates across various LLMs as the number of disks is increased.

### 4.4 Solving the 20-disk problem: Over one million steps with zero errors4.4 解决 20 盘问题：一百多万步且零错误

With gpt-4.1-mini as the base model, the maximum output token threshold was set to 750, and a red-flagging output parser was used to enforce the basic formatting requirements. Beyond this cutoff, the model often found itself in confusing logical loops; misformatted output was often correlated with such twisted reasoning, allowing them to be detected and discarded (as will be discussed further in Section  [4.5](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.09030v1#S4.SS5 "4.5 Investigating the impact of red-flagging ‣ 4 Experiments ‣ Solving a Million-Step LLM Task with Zero Errors")). Since $k_{\min}=3$ , at least three responses were generated in parallel for each step. The first step was generated with a temperature of 0 to make sure the best possible guess is included in the votes. For all subsequent votes, a temperature of 0.1 was used.  
以 gpt-4.1-mini 作为基础模型，最大输出令牌阈值设置为 750，且使用了红旗输出解析器以强制执行基本格式要求。超过此截止点后，模型常常陷入令人困惑的逻辑循环；格式错误的输出常与这种扭曲的推理相关联，从而可以被检测并丢弃（在第 4.5 节将进一步讨论）。由于 $k_{\min}=3$ ，每一步至少并行生成三份响应。第一步使用温度 0 生成，以确保投票中包含最佳可能猜测。对于后续所有投票，使用了温度 0.1。

### 4.5 Investigating the impact of red-flagging4.5 调查标记为高风险的影响

Red-flagging was hypothesized to reduce the per-step error rate, but also the impact of correlated errors, i.e., particular steps that have unusually high error rates compared to the average step. ## 5 Discussion and Future Work5 讨论与未来工作

This paper introduced a framework for massively decomposed agentic processes (MDAPs) that can reliably solve tasks with large numbers of steps, as well as the first implementation of this framework, MAKER, which was successfully applied to the Towers of Hanoi benchmark task. This initial study established core principles that open many directions for future work.  
本文提出了一个用于大规模分解代理过程（MDAPs）的框架，该框架能够可靠地解决具有大量步骤的任务，并介绍了该框架的第一个实现——MAKER，已成功应用于汉诺塔基准任务。该初步研究确立了核心原则，为未来工作开辟了许多方向。

#### More General Applications更通用的应用场景

LLM behaviors can be divided into two categories: *insights* and *execution*. Insights come from an LLM creatively generating ideas, plans, and strategies, while execution involves following through with them. This paper focused on execution: the overall strategy to solve the problem is set at the beginning of the process, and given this strategy, the answer to each subtask is clearly achievable. Extending the framework to handle LLM-based insights is an important area of future work, since insights are inherently more open-ended and may come with irreducible step-wise uncertainty. One concrete way is to apply MAKER to the case where the creation of each subtask is itself treated as a step in an overall decomposition. The goal is to automate the entire problem-solving pipeline: the task is decomposed into minimal chunks, each one is solved, and the results are aggregated into a complete solution. The framework needs to be extended to handle an unknown number of total steps, as well as steps of different types, different underlying success rates, inexact matches between insight steps, and possible failures of the matching process.  
LLM 的行为可以分为两类：洞见和执行。洞见来自 LLM 创造性地生成想法、计划和策略，而执行则涉及将这些想法付诸实施。本文侧重于执行：解决问题的总体策略在流程开始时就已确定，基于该策略，每个子任务的答案都是可清晰实现的。将该框架扩展到处理基于 LLM 的洞见是未来工作的一个重要方向，因为洞见本质上更具开放性，可能伴随不可约的逐步不确定性。一种具体方法是将 MAKER 应用于这样一种情况：每个子任务的创造本身被视为整体分解中的一个步骤。目标是自动化整个问题解决流程：将任务分解为最小块，分别解决每一块，并将结果汇总为完整的解决方案。该框架需要扩展以处理未知数量的总步骤，以及不同类型的步骤、不同的基础成功率、洞见步骤之间的不精确匹配和匹配过程可能的失败。

Preliminary experiments in this direction are promising (Appendix [F](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.09030v1#A6 "Appendix F Multiplication Experiments ‣ Appendix E Open-source Model Details ‣ Appendix D Sample Responses ‣ Appendix C Prompts and Parsers ‣ Solving a Million-Step LLM Task with Zero Errors")). A more general version of MAKER was created with four agent types: decomposition agents, called recursively to break a task into two simpler sub-tasks and a composition function; decomposition discriminator agents, called to vote (with first-to- $k$ voting) for one of $n=2k-1$ decomposition candidates; solution discriminator agents, called to vote for one of $n$ composition candidates; and problem solver agents used to solve minimal subtasks without decomposing them. The system achieved promising results on large-digit multiplication, a notoriously difficult task for transformer-based models \[Dziriexp, bai2025canttransformerslearnmultiplication\]. Future work will investigate the broader potential of such a system.  
在这一方向上的初步实验很有前景（附录 F）。一个更通用版本的 MAKER 被创建出来，具有四种代理类型：分解代理（递归调用以将任务分解为两个更简单的子任务和一个组合函数）；分解判别代理（被调用以对 $n=2k-1$ 个分解候选中的一个进行投票，采用先到为 $k$ 的投票规则）；解答判别代理（被调用以对 $n$ 个组合候选中的一个进行投票）；以及问题求解代理（用于在不分解的情况下解决最小子任务）。该系统在大数位乘法上取得了有希望的结果，这一任务对基于 Transformer 的模型来说向来非常困难 \[Dziriexp, bai2025canttransformerslearnmultiplication\]。未来的工作将探讨此类系统的更广泛潜力。

For simplicity, in this paper, all MAKER agents used the same underlying LLM and their prompts only differed in the subtask they were assigned. More general systems will likely require different LLMs for different kinds of roles, and a general increased diversity across agents. One of the benefits of such diversity will be decorrelation of errors, as is discussed next.  
为简洁起见，本文中所有 MAKER 代理都使用相同的底层 LLM，它们的提示仅在所分配的子任务上有所不同。更通用的系统很可能需要针对不同角色使用不同的 LLM，并在代理之间普遍增加多样性。此类多样性的其中一个好处是能够使错误去相关化，下一节将对此进行讨论。

#### The Importance of Decorrelated Errors去相关错误的重要性

For clarity and simplicity, theoretical analysis in this paper assumed that the errors are i.i.d. across steps. This assumption was reasonable because the steps were relatively uniform. Even so, there were a few steps that, for no apparent reason, had substantially higher inherent error rates than others. Such strange behaviors for particular inputs are well-known side-effects of LLM training, and dealing with such correlated errors is an open foundational problem in machine learning \[lehman2025evolution\].  
为清晰和简洁起见，本文的理论分析假设各步骤之间的错误是独立同分布的（i.i.d.）。这一假设是合理的，因为各步骤相对均匀。即便如此，也有少数步骤在没有明显原因的情况下，其固有错误率显著高于其他步骤。针对特定输入出现此类奇怪行为是 LLM 训练的已知副作用，而处理此类相关错误是机器学习中的一个尚未解决的基础性问题 \[lehman2025evolution\]。

The independent sampling plus red-flagging method used in this paper was sufficient to overcome them, but there may be other real-world cases where more sophisticated decorrelation methods are required. For example, instead of simple resampling using temperature, paraphrasing the prompt \[wahle2024paraphrase\] or adding noise to the prompt in some other way could help avoid such anomalous states caused by a particular fixed context. The error rate of each step would then approach the true ability of the LLM to understand and execute that step. Further, the framework could be extended to account for different values of $p$ for different steps, and decorrelation methods that make sampling more effective. Such extensions are critical to make the framework more broadly applicable, since in a long-range task, even a single step with an abnormally high error rate can cause the reasoning process to fail.  
本文中使用的独立抽样加红旗标记方法足以克服这些问题，但在其他现实场景中可能需要更复杂的去相关方法。例如，与其简单地使用温度重采样，不如对提示进行改写\[wahle2024paraphrase\]或以其他方式向提示添加噪声，这有助于避免由某个固定上下文引起的异常状态。这样每一步的错误率将更接近 LLM 理解并执行该步骤的真实能力。此外，该框架可以扩展以考虑不同步骤的不同 $p$ 值，以及使抽样更有效的去相关方法。这样的扩展对于使框架更广泛适用至关重要，因为在一个长期任务中，即使只有一步的错误率异常高，也可能导致推理过程失败。

#### Parallels with microservices与微服务的相似之处

Parallels can be drawn between microagents and microservices. The benefits of decomposing a monolithic agent’s task into subtasks are similar to those of decomposing a monolithic application into smaller services \[fowler\_microservices\]:  
微代理与微服务之间可以找到类比。将单体代理的任务分解为子任务的好处类似于将单体应用分解为更小服务的好处\[fowler\_microservices\]：

- Modularity: Each microagent can be tailored to a specific task and leverage the right tools for the job.
	  
	• 模块化：每个微代理都可以针对特定任务进行定制，并使用适合该任务的工具。
- Data management: Each microagent is responsible for managing its data.
	  
	• 数据管理：每个微代理负责管理自己的数据。
- Independent development: Microagents can be updated and tested in isolation from the rest of the system.
	  
	• 独立开发：微代理可以在与系统其余部分隔离的情况下进行更新和测试。
- Scalability: Microagents can be scaled independently, adjusting the resources to the actual needs of the system.
	  
	• 可扩展性：微代理可以独立扩展，根据系统的实际需求调整资源。
- Communication: Natural language is a powerful, well-understood communication protocol.
	  
	• 通信：自然语言是一种强大且被广泛理解的通信协议。
- Complexity: As microservices solve for large-scale systems, microagents solve for complex reasoning tasks.
	  
	• 复杂性：正如微服务解决大规模系统，微代理（microagents）解决复杂的推理任务。
- Real-time monitoring: Microagents can be monitored in real-time.
	  
	• 实时监控：微代理可以实时监控。
- Design for failure: Microagents are designed to tolerate the failure of any of the agents.
	  
	• 为失败而设计：微代理被设计为容忍任何代理的故障。
- Evolutionary design: Change is easier to manage with microagents than with a monolithic agent.
	  
	• 进化式设计：与单一大型代理相比，微代理更容易管理变更。

In fact, microagents could be considered a natural evolution of microservices. The framework could be extended in that direction, leveraging the lessons learned from microservice architectures \[goyal2025microservices\].  
事实上，微代理可以被视为微服务的自然演进。该框架可以朝这个方向扩展，借鉴微服务架构中获得的经验教训\[goyal2025microservices\]。

#### Limits of Decomposition 分解的局限

The application of MAKER assumes a task can be decomposed into small enough and simple enough steps such that each step can be solved by an LLM agent with reasonable probability. There is thus one central question that will dictate how broadly the methods can be applied: Are there important problems where such a decomposition is not possible or is computationally infeasible to discover? At the lowest level of LLM implementation, there is a decomposition into primitive operations executed on CPUs or GPUs; one can hope that there is some decomposition between that and the entire problem that is still linguistic but effectively compartmentalizes context and different behaviors. It remains to be seen which kinds of tasks are most resistant to such a decomposition.  
MAKER 的应用假定任务可以被分解为足够小且足够简单的步骤，以致每个步骤都能由 LLM 代理以合理概率解决。因此，有一个核心问题将决定这些方法能被广泛应用的程度：是否存在重要问题无法进行这样的分解，或发现这样的分解在计算上不可行？在 LLM 实现的最低层，有一个分解为在 CPU 或 GPU 上执行的原始操作；人们可以期望在那一层与整个问题之间存在某种分解，这种分解仍然是语言层面的，但能有效地将上下文和不同的行为进行区隔。目前尚不清楚哪类任务对这种分解最具抵抗力。

#### Safety, Morality, and the Future of Superintelligence安全性、道德性与超智能的未来

If large and important real-world problems can be successfully decomposed into microsteps, there could be major benefits with respect to safety. If each step has a clearly defined and limited focus and purpose, the LLM’s view of the world and domain of influence can be strictly limited, allowing for more effective sand-boxing, auditing, and general control. Multiple focused agents can be run independently on each step, which also substantially reduces the ability of agents to collude to produce harmful actions. As was seen in the experiments in this paper, the vast majority of work can be performed by relatively small LLMs that are capable of handling these small steps, thus avoiding the risks of harmful behaviors that can arise in more powerful models \[lynch2025agentic\]. In other words, it could help mitigate the risk of uncontrollable superintelligence. Complementing these reduced societal risks, extreme decomposition could reduce the chance of unintended suffering of the machines themselves, as model welfare has become an increased area of concern \[anthropic2025claude4systemcard\]. As argued in prior work, relying on smaller models and having models focus entirely on limited-scope subtasks, as is done through decomposition, could reduce the chance that sentience unintentionally emerges \[meyersonposition, tkachenko2024position\].  
如果大型且重要的现实问题能够成功分解为微步骤，那么在安全性方面可能带来重大好处。如果每个步骤都有明确定义且有限的关注点和目的，LLM 对世界和影响范围的视角可以被严格限制，从而实现更有效的沙箱化、审计和整体控制。可以在每个步骤上独立运行多个聚焦型代理，这也大大降低了代理合谋产生有害行为的能力。正如本文实验所示，大部分工作可以由能够处理这些小步骤的相对较小的 LLM 完成，从而避免在更强大模型中可能出现的有害行为风险 \[lynch2025agentic\]。换句话说，这可能有助于减轻不可控的风险superintelligence.。除了这些降低的社会风险外，极端分解还可能减少机器自身意外受苦的可能性，因为模型福利已成为一个日益关注的领域\[anthropic2025claude4systemcard\]。 正如先前研究所论证的那样，依赖更小的模型并让模型完全专注于范围有限的子任务（正如通过分解所做的那样），可以降低感知意外出现的可能性 \[meyersonposition, tkachenko2024position\]。

LLMs today have just about all the raw intelligence needed to scaffold them into the great superintelligent skyscrapers of the coming age, and to scaffold themselves into productive organizations of technological progress. MDAPs present an alternative path to realizing the benefits of superintelligence, which, compared to endlessly building bigger and smarter single-agent models, comes with substantially reduced risks to both humans and machines.  
当今的 LLMs 几乎具备了将其支撑成未来那个超智能摩天大楼所需的全部原始智能，并能将自己搭建成推动技术进步的高效组织。与不断构建更大、更聪明的单体模型相比，MDAPs 提供了一条实现超智能好处的替代路径，其对人类和机器的风险显著降低。

## 6 Conclusion 6 结论

This paper focused on the question of how LLM-based agentic systems can be massively scaled. Decomposing tasks into minimal subtasks makes it possible to apply error-correction techniques effectively and efficiently, supporting scaling to millions of steps and beyond. A new category of AI systems results, i.e. massively decomposed agentic processes, or MDAPs. MAKER is a first implementation of this approach, and the experiments in this paper on Towers of Hanoi a first demonstration of its value. This foundation opens the door to more general-purpose implementations and large-scale, long-running real-world applications. Such MDAPs offer an alternative to building endlessly larger and more intelligent LLMs: By smashing intelligence into a million pieces, it is possible to build AI that is efficient, safe, and reliable.  
本文聚焦于如何将基于 LLM 的具代理性系统大规模扩展这一问题。将任务分解为最小的子任务，使得对错误进行纠正的技术可以高效且有效地应用，从而支持扩展到数百万步及更高。由此产生了一类新的 AI 系统，即大规模分解的具代理性过程，或称 MDAPs。MAKER 是这一方法的首个实现，本文在汉诺塔问题上的实验首次展示了其价值。这个基础为更通用的实现以及大规模、长时间运行的现实应用打开了大门。这类 MDAPs 为构建无止境地更大更智能的 LLMs 提供了另一种选择：通过将智能碎裂成一百万块，可以构建出高效、安全且可靠的 AI。
