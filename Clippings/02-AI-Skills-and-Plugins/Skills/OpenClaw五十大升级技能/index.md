**Skills are the body. Always tell your OpenClaw to watch the skill you are going to download by SkillGuard to ensure your environment stays secure while you scale.**

Technically, a Skill is just a packaged instruction set (usually a [SKILL.md](https://skill.md/) file) that tells your agent exactly how to use a specific tool or follow a complex workflow.

Instead of saying: "Please look at my code and tell me if there's an error," a skill-equipped agent says: "I'm going to use my debug-pro skill to run a systematic sweep of your repo, check the logs, and fix the syntax in your terminal."

**Skills solve this.** They allow OpenClaw to load only the specific "playbook" it needs for the task at hand. It keeps the agent sharp, the costs low, and the results repeatable.
