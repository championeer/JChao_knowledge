All tech companies have to choose what to build, but approaches vary — from top-down projects to bottom-up research and experimentation. Your company is probably somewhere on this gamut, but it’s not always a deliberate choice. It’s important to realize that these are not the only two possible models, and that there isn’t necessarily just one right answer.  
所有科技公司都必须选择要构建什么，但方法各不相同——从自上而下的项目到自下而上的研究与实验。你的公司很可能处在这个光谱的某个位置，但这并不总是一个刻意的选择。重要的是要意识到，这并非仅有的两种模型，也不一定只有一个正确答案。

In this article I’ll lay out the territory of product discovery using consultants’ favorite tool: the 2×2 grid.  
在这篇文章中，我将使用顾问们最喜欢的工具——2×2 网格，来描绘产品探索的版图。

## The Two Axes of Product Discovery产品发现的两条轴线

We can map product discovery along these axes:  
我们可以沿着这些轴线来绘制产品发现的地图：

- **Centralized vs. Empowered**: Who’s driving the process? Is discovery owned by a central authority (e.g. leadership, a founder, or a select group), or are teams trusted to make local product decisions?  
	集中式与授权式：谁在推动这个过程？发现是由中央权威（例如领导层、创始人或一个选定的小组）拥有，还是信任团队做出本地产品决策？
- **Opinion-based vs. Evidence-guided**: Are decisions made based on gut feel, consensus, and rank, or grounded in data, analysis, and experiments?  
	基于观点与以证据为导向：决策是基于直觉、共识和职级，还是以数据、分析和实验为依据？

These axes form four quadrants, each with its own models, advantages, and challenges.  
这些轴线形成了四个象限，每个象限都有其自身的模型、优点和挑战。

![](https://itamargilad.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Product-discovery-grid-1.jpg)

## 1\. Centralized + Opinion-Based: Command-and-Control1. 集中式 + 基于观点：指挥与控制

In this quadrant the assumption is that some people are able to predict which products and business ideas will succeed and which will fail, which implementation is correct and which is wrong.  
在这个象限中，假定某些人能够预测哪些产品和商业想法会成功、哪些会失败，以及哪种实现是正确的、哪种是错误的。

The classic example is the traditional company performing what I call the **planning waterfall** —leaders or committees define strategies, roadmaps, and projects, mostly based on sparse data and opinions; “delivery-teams” execute.  
经典的例子是我所称的传统公司执行的计划瀑布——领导或委员会制定战略、路线图和项目，主要基于稀疏的数据和观点；“交付团队”负责执行。

![](https://itamargilad.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/Planning-Waterfall-1024x411.jpg)

But there are more modern takes on this theme. When I worked at Microsoft in the 2000s we practiced **classic product management**, where PMs were entrusted with roadmaps, project plans, and detailed specs. We created those mostly based on our own judgement and the opinions and feedback of our colleagues and managers. I still see this form of product management practiced widely today.  
但也有对这一主题的更现代的看法。2000 年代我在微软工作时，我们奉行经典的产品管理模式，产品经理被赋予路线图、项目计划和详细规格的职责。我们主要基于自己的判断以及同事和经理的意见与反馈来制定这些内容。我至今仍看到这种产品管理形式被广泛实践。

In recent years, some successful silicon valley entrepreneurs have been promoting the concept of [**Founder Mode**](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/itamargilad_beware-of-founder-mode-activity-7242914347130249217-EAvG/) (aka **founder-led companies**). The core belief is founders are unique, visionary people that should run their companies in a radically centralized way — essentially making or approving all product decisions. Empowerment, A/B testing, and other modern practices are considered harmful and befitting of “professional managers”. The inspiration for founder mode? “Steve Jobs” (I’ll touch on this myth later in the article).  
近年来，一些成功的硅谷创业者在推广“创始人模式”（又名创始人主导公司）的概念。其核心信念是创始人是独特的、有远见的人，应该以极度集中的方式管理公司——本质上由他们制定或批准所有产品决策。赋权、A/B 测试以及其他现代做法被认为有害且只适合“职业经理人”。创始人模式的灵感来源？“史蒂夫·乔布斯”（我将在文章后面谈到这个神话）。

There’s a kernel of truth here — leaders should indeed lead and guide the company, and “democracy” (which the founder-mode people seem to fear the most) doesn’t work for product development. Still, as I’ve [written in the past](https://itamargilad.com/decisions-part1/), trusting all decisions to a select group of people is very risky:  
这里面有一丝真理——领导者确实应该领导并指导公司，而“民主”（创始人模式的人似乎最害怕的东西）并不适用于产品开发。不过，正如我过去所写，把所有决策都委托给少数人是非常冒险的：

> This centralized approach to decision-making may be better for consistency and alignment, and may feel less risky, but it can, and often does, lead to bottlenecks, slow decisions, high meeting overhead, and disempowered, demoralized employees. Worse, relying on the snap-judgment of busy people that may be optimistic and self-confident, but not always well informed, is hardly a recipe for great decision-making.  
> 这种集中式的决策方法在一致性和对齐方面可能更好，感觉上风险更小，但它会并且经常导致瓶颈、决策缓慢、会议开销大，以及员工被剥夺权力、士气低落。更糟的是，依赖那些忙碌人士的快速判断——他们可能乐观且自信，但并不总是掌握充分信息——几乎不是良好决策的配方。  

## 2\. Empowered + Opinion-Based: Creative Chaos2. 授权 + 基于观点：创造性的混乱

It’s been shown time and again that rank-and-file employees, especially engineers, are able to come up with some of the best product ideas — Gmail, Twitter, and Slack are famous examples. The aim in this quadrant is to tap into this creative power by allowing individuals and teams to pursue product ideas without having to go through rigorous validation or approval processes.  
一次又一次的研究表明，普通员工，尤其是工程师，能够提出一些最好的产品创意——Gmail、Twitter 和 Slack 就是著名例子。本象限的目标是通过允许个人和团队在不必经过严格验证或审批流程的情况下追求产品想法，来挖掘这种创造力。

This approach was quite trendy in the past. Google is famous for instituting the 20% project policy and generally encouraging bottom-up innovation. Some projects graduated into betas and full-fledged products, but most were sunset due to lack of usage. This “throwing stuff at the wall” approach helped Google create an ecosystem of web services that attracted loyal users and accelerated loyalty and growth.  
这种方法在过去非常流行。谷歌以推行 20%项目政策并普遍鼓励自下而上的创新而闻名。一些项目升级为测试版乃至成熟产品，但大多数由于缺乏使用而被淘汰。这种“把东西扔到墙上看哪个能粘住”的做法帮助谷歌创建了一个吸引忠实用户并加速忠诚度与增长的网络服务生态系统。

![](https://itamargilad.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Google-projects-early-years-1024x644.png)

Google products 1998-2005 (partial list)

But the Creative Chaos approach is not without its disadvantages. The company has to be merciless at killing ideas that don’t work (“fail fast” was one of Google’s mantras as demonstrated by its extensive [graveyard](https://killedbygoogle.com/)) or suffer an excess of incompatible apps and services. Creative chaos works when you have entrepreneurial, well-informed employees, and an environment that allows bottom-up ideas to compete for funding with top-down ideas. Both are not true for most organizations. It’s also harder to manage and align this form of product discovery, and it may distract from the mission and strategy of the company.  
但“创造性混沌”方法并非没有缺点。公司必须无情地淘汰那些不起作用的想法（“快速失败”是谷歌的格言之一，其大量的墓地就说明了这一点），否则就会遭遇大量彼此不兼容的应用和服务。创造性混沌在你拥有富有创业精神、信息充足的员工，并且允许自下而上的想法与自上而下的想法争夺资金的环境中才有效。而这两点对大多数组织而言都不成立。以这种方式进行产品发现也更难管理和协调，而且可能会分散公司在使命和战略上的注意力。

For these reasons creative chaos is far less popular today. When I joined Google in 2010, the senior leaders were already shifting the culture towards top-down big-bet projects. The inspiration? “Steve Jobs” (wrong again as I’ll explain shortly). But the spirit of bottom-up intuitive product discovery still lives in Google and in many other companies through side projects, hackathons, internal startup incubators, and innovation labs. It’s a powerful product discovery pattern, but rarely the main one.  
出于这些原因，创造性混乱今天变得不那么受欢迎了。2010 年我加入谷歌时，高层领导已经在把文化转向自上而下的大赌注项目。灵感来源？“史蒂夫·乔布斯”（我随后会解释为什么这是错误的）。但自下而上的直觉式产品发现精神仍在谷歌以及许多其他公司中以旁支项目、黑客马拉松、内部初创孵化器和创新实验室的形式存在。这是一种强有力的产品发现模式，但很少成为主要方式。

## 3\. Centralized + Evidence-Guided: Benevolent Dictatorships3. 集中式 + 以证据为导向：仁慈的独裁

> *“If we have data, let’s look at data. If all we have are opinions, let’s go with mine.”* — Jim Barksdale, co-founder of Netscape  
> “如果我们有数据，就看数据。如果我们只有意见，那就听我的。”——Jim Barksdale，网景联合创始人

The leaders in this quadrant are very much in charge, but they acknowledge they are fallible and wish for their assertions to be tested. Evidence weighs heavier even than manager opinions.  
该象限的领导者掌控力很强，但他们承认自己可能犯错，并希望自己的断言接受检验。证据的分量甚至超过了经理的观点。

Judging by his words and actions, Steve Jobs believed in this form of product discovery:  
从言行来看，Steve Jobs 相信这种形式的产品探索：

![](https://itamargilad.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Steve-Jobs-Video-1024x645.jpg)

> *“A lot of people will tell you that I had a very strong opinion and they presented evidence to the contrary and five minutes later I completely changed my mind, because I’m like that. I don’t mind being wrong and I’ll admit that I’m wrong a lot. It doesn’t really matter to me too much, what matters to me is that we do the right thing.” —* Steve Jobs ([video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=i-nuoHWhCEw))  
> “很多人会告诉你，我曾有很强的观点，有人拿出相反的证据来，五分钟后我就完全改变了主意，因为我就是这样的。我不介意自己错了，也会承认自己经常错。对我来说这并不是最重要的，重要的是我们做对的事。”—— 史蒂夫·乔布斯（视频）

These are not just empty words: Jobs initially didn’t believe in multitouch, was against Apple developing a phone, and did not want to launch an app store, but he allowed others to convince him otherwise. All of these were bottom-up ideas that Jobs didn’t initiate, but he came to accept.  
这些话不是空谈：乔布斯最初并不相信多点触控，反对苹果开发手机，也不想推出应用商店，但他允许别人说服他改变主意。所有这些都是自下而上的想法，乔布斯并非发起者，但他最终接受了。

The benevolent dictator model offers a powerful mix of human judgment and evidence-guided decision, which is why it is used at Meta, Amazon, and other bigtechs; still it’s not applicable to most companies:  
仁慈独裁者模型将人类判断与证据驱动决策有力地结合在一起，这就是为什么 Meta、Amazon 和其他大型科技公司采用它；但它并不适用于大多数公司：

- Relies on having extremely capable senior leaders that are well informed, technology and business savvy, yet humble in the face of contradicting data  
	依赖于拥有极其有能力的高级领导者，他们信息灵通、既懂技术又懂业务，但在面对相互矛盾的数据时仍保持谦逊
- Does not scale a— the leaders can be closely involved only with a select few projects (known in Facebook as “being in the eye of Sauron”). Everything else has to be operated in a different way  
	无法扩展——这些领导者只能密切参与为数不多的项目（在 Facebook 内被称为“处于索伦之眼”）。其他所有事务都必须以不同方式运作
- Still puts all the decisions in the hands of a few powerful people which may lead to false positives (the Metaverses, Amazon Fire phone) and false negatives.  
	仍将所有决策交由少数有权势的人掌控，这可能导致假阳性（如元宇宙、亚马逊 Fire 手机）和假阴性。

## 4\. Empowered + Evidence-Guided: The Product Operating Model4. 授权 + 证据驱动：产品运营模型

![](https://itamargilad.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/Discovery-books-1024x285.jpg)

This approach is considered the gold standard by many product thinkers, consultants, and practitioners. It’s heavily inspired by the scientific method, modern business management theory, psychology and sociology.  
这种方法被许多产品思想者、顾问和从业者视为金标准。它深受科学方法、现代商业管理理论、心理学和社会学的启发。

The product operating model (a term coined by Marty Cagan) calls for leaders to develop a strategy and define concise measurable goals. Product teams, with help of managers, translate those into tactical goals and then conduct research, evaluate and test multiple ideas, and build and launch the ideas that move the needle. There’s room for bottom-up goals and bottom up ideas. Top-down big bets exist, but they start small, and are subject to the same validation as other ideas. All decisions are made using a combination of human judgement and evidence.  
产品运营模型（Marty Cagan 首创的术语）要求领导者制定战略并定义简明的可衡量目标。产品团队在经理的帮助下将这些目标转化为战术性目标，然后开展研究、评估和测试多个想法，并构建和发布那些真正推动指标变化的想法。该模型允许自下而上的目标和自下而上的想法。自上而下的大赌注也存在，但它们从小处开始，并像其他想法一样接受相同的验证。所有决策都结合了人的判断与证据。

There are clear benefits to this approach. Companies that employ the product operating model can drop their bad ideas early and double-down on the ideas that work (which can truly [10x outcomes](https://itamargilad.com/velocity-vs-impact/)). Leaders at all levels are liberated of the burden of having to tell people exactly what to do. The quick iteration mode of build-measure-learn is much more engaging and rewarding for engineers, designers, and product managers. Leaders find they can achieve more having  [value creation teams rather than feature factories](https://itamargilad.com/feature-factory/).  
这种方法有明显的好处。采用产品运营模型的公司可以及早放弃糟糕的想法，并加倍投入那些有效的想法（这确实能把成果提升十倍）。各级领导不再背负必须告诉他人确切该做什么的负担。快速的构建—测量—学习迭代模式对工程师、设计师和产品经理而言更有吸引力且更有成就感。领导者会发现，与其维持功能工厂，不如建立价值创造团队能取得更大的成就。

The challenges of this approach are also substantial. Empowerment and evidence-guided development go against long held beliefs and practices. The [full product operating model](https://itamargilad.com/product-operating-model/) requires the organization to uplevel its strategy, goals, research, dev infrastructure, and culture. In practice, many companies get stuck in some hybrid of the new and old.  
这种方法的挑战也相当巨大。赋权和以证据为导向的开发违背了长期以来的信念和做法。完整的产品运营模型要求组织在战略、目标、研究、开发基础设施和文化上实现升级。实际上，许多公司在新旧之间某种混合状态中陷入僵局。

## It’s Not That Simple 事情没那么简单

I’m not a big fan of 2×2 matrices because the world rarely splits neatly into four clear-cut cases. The same is true for product discovery. Many companies are operating in a hybrid mode that spans across the grid. Take Amazon for example — teams are empowered to come up with ideas and run fast local experiments, but on bigger matters they have to go through the chain of command using the rigorous PR/FAQ review process. Most big initiatives still come top-down and managers keep a close watch on progress.  
我不太喜欢 2×2 矩阵，因为世界很少能被整齐地划分为四个明确的案例。产品发现也是如此。许多公司都在跨越这个网格的混合模式中运作。以亚马逊为例——团队被赋予权力提出想法并快速开展本地实验，但在更重大问题上他们必须通过指挥链，使用严格的 PR/FAQ 评审流程。大多数重大举措仍然是自上而下的，管理者对进展保持密切关注。

Gen-AI paradoxically is reinforcing all beliefs. Tech bros see the AI sea change as a reason to double-down on their founder-led worldview — how else can they “act with urgency”? Some believe that vibe-coding will create another wave of Creative Chaos where anyone can make changes to the product (good luck with that). Top-down traditionalists see AI as a way to amplify the output of their feature factories. Evidence-guided development enthusiasts like myself believe that AI will help us [overcome human biases](https://itamargilad.com/ai-usecases/) and make us better at [observing the world and testing our ideas](https://itamargilad.com/ai-pm/). It’ll likely take years to discover the real impact on product innovation.  
生成式人工智能具有悖论性，它在强化所有信念。科技大佬们把这场人工智能的变革视为加倍坚持他们以创始人为中心世界观的理由——不然他们如何“以紧迫感行事”？有人认为氛围编码（vibe-coding）会掀起另一波创造性混乱，在那种情况下任何人都可以对产品做出改动（祝你好运）。自上而下的传统主义者则把人工智能视为放大他们功能工厂产出的手段。像我这样的证据导向开发拥护者认为，人工智能将帮助我们克服人类偏见，让我们更善于观察世界并检验我们的想法。要弄清它对产品创新的真正影响，可能需要多年时间。

For now, I hope this model will be a useful conversation starter — where are you today on these two axes and where do you want to end up (tip: if you do nothing you’ll likely slide towards Command and Control, that’s just how it is). As always, if you found this article useful please share with the people that ought to know and take action and with your broader networks.  
目前，我希望这个模型能成为一个有用的对话起点——你现在在这两条坐标轴上的位置是哪里，想要到达哪儿（提示：如果你什么都不做，很可能会滑向指挥与控制，那就是事实）。一如既往，如果你觉得这篇文章有用，请与那些应该知道并付诸行动的人以及你更广泛的关系网络分享。
